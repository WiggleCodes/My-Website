Private Storage Service Agreement & Terms of Service

Reeces-IT — private cloud storage on a home server

Draft template — not legal advice. See the note at the end of this page before relying on it.

1. The parties

This agreement is between Reece Feetenby, trading as "Reeces-IT" ("the Provider", "I", "me"), and the individual purchasing a storage plan ("the Customer", "you"). By paying for and using the service, you agree to these terms.

2. What the service is

Reeces-IT provides private, encrypted cloud storage hosted on the Provider's own network-attached storage (NAS) server(s), located in the UK. The service is intended for personal backup and storage of files such as photos, videos, and documents, accessible from the Customer's own devices.

4. Payment & term

  • Plans are billed monthly and renew automatically unless cancelled.
  • Payment is due at the start of each billing period; access may be suspended if payment fails and is not resolved within 7 days.
  • The Customer may cancel at any time; cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period, with no partial refund for the remaining days.
  • The Provider may change prices with at least 30 days' notice to existing Customers.

5. Data protection & privacy

The Provider will take reasonable technical measures to keep Customer data private and encrypted, and will not access, scan, sell, or share Customer files, and will not use them for advertising or profiling.

Because this service involves storing personal data (such as photos containing identifiable people) on the Provider's infrastructure, both parties should be aware that UK data protection law (UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018) may apply. See the note at the end of this page — this is flagged for the Provider to check, not resolved by this template.

6. Acceptable use

The Customer agrees not to use the storage space to store or distribute:

  • Illegal content of any kind, including material that infringes copyright or third-party rights.
  • Malware, or files intended to disrupt or gain unauthorised access to systems.
  • Content that harasses, threatens, or endangers others.

The Provider reserves the right to suspend or terminate access, without refund, for a clear breach of this section.

7. Backups, availability & data loss

Files are archived in addition to the primary copy held on the server. However, this is a home-based service run on a limited number of physical machines, not a redundant, geographically distributed data centre. The Provider does not guarantee 100% uptime or that data can never be lost (for example, in the event of simultaneous hardware failure, fire, flood, or theft at the Provider's premises).

The Customer is strongly encouraged to keep an independent copy of anything irreplaceable and not to rely on Reeces-IT as a sole backup for critical files.

8. Security

  • Access to Customer storage is private and access-controlled.
  • Data is encrypted; specific encryption methods may be shared with the Customer on request.
  • The Customer is responsible for keeping their own login credentials secure.

9. Termination & suspension

Either party may end this agreement at any time. The Provider may suspend or terminate an account immediately for breach of the acceptable use policy, non-payment, or if continuing to provide the service becomes impractical (for example, hardware failure or discontinuing the service). Where reasonably possible, the Provider will give notice and an opportunity for the Customer to retrieve their files before deletion.

10. Limitation of liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Provider's liability for any loss arising from use of the service (including data loss or service downtime) is limited to the fees paid by the Customer in the 3 months prior to the event giving rise to the claim. This does not exclude liability that cannot be limited under law, such as liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, or fraud.

11. Changes to these terms

The Provider may update these terms from time to time, for example to reflect new plans, additional servers, or changes to how the service is run. Material changes will be communicated to active Customers in advance of taking effect.

12. Governing law

This agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales, and both parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

Before you rely on this

This is a starting template, not a finished legal document, and I'm not a lawyer. Two things worth getting proper advice on before relying on it:

1) Data protection — storing other people's personal data (photos, documents) as a service may mean you need to register with the ICO and meet UK GDPR obligations around security, breach notification, and data subject rights. Worth checking directly, especially since this is run personally rather than through a registered company.

2) Consumer contract law — if any customers are individuals buying for personal use, UK consumer protection law (e.g. rights around cancellation and service quality) applies on top of whatever this page says. A solicitor or a small-business legal advisor can confirm the wording holds up.
